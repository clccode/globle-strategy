The "Globle" Strategy Page

Globle is a great game! It's name is a play on the popular word game Wordle but it's quite different.

It will test how well you know geography. However, arriving at the correct answer each day can be a long process. While geography buffs will always do well, consider the math. Globle has 196 countries (note that Globle includes Taiwan as a possible answer). That gives you about a .05% chance of guessing it right on the first try. Those aren't good odds.

It helps to think in terms of probability in order to help you get to the right answer faster. Here is the breakdown of the number of countries in the world by region:

  • Africa: 54 countries
  • Asia: 49 countries
  • Europe: 44 countries
  • Latin America & the Caribbean: 33 countries
  • Oceania: 14 countries
  • North America: 2 countries
Notice where the majority of the countries in the world are. Fifty-four (27.5%) of the world's countries are in Africa. Forty-nine (25%) of the countries in the world are in Asia (note: that includes the Middle East region). Europe has 44 countries, or 22.4% of the total. That give you a clue where to make your first guess.

With a slightly better than 1 in 4 chance that the country is in Africa, that is a good continent to draw your first guess from. Asia, with a 1 in 4 chance of having the solution, is also a good choice. Both are very large continents, so if the answer you put in shows > 5000 km, it's likely that the solution is in another region/continent.

A recent example

Here is how I solved Globle on June 16, 2022:

  • 1st guess: Tunisia (2140 km from the solution)
  • 2nd guess: Eritrea (1340 km from the solution)
  • 3rd guess: Central African Republic (farther, no km given if further from the answer)
  • 4th guess: Kenya (also further from the solution)
Given I started in North Africa and the sub-Saharan African countries I selected were further from the solution, I figured it was time to move to a different continent. Given the first guess, Tunisia, was 2140 km from the solution, I guessed the solution was likely in the Middle East (Asia). Here's how I continued:
  • 5th guess: Syria (< 10 km from the solution). This tells me the answer is a country that borders Syria.
  • 6th guess: Israel--the correct answer!
Getting the correct answer in 6 guesses isn't bad, but it's not easy to do.

Take a look at the map of Syria below:

When you look at the map, I had 5 countries to choose from: Lebanon, Israel, Jordan, Iraq, and Turkey. I had a 1 in 5 chance of getting it right (20%) and I made the right choice. It also could have gone the other way and I make four more guesses until I find the answer.

Once you narrow it down is when your knowledge of geography comes into play. Africa, for example, has 54 countries and it is hard to know exactly where they all are. Also, if the solution is one of the small Pacific or Caribbean islands it might take a lot of turns to find the right answer. That's what makes the game so challenging.

I hope you enjoyed this short tutorial. Globle is a difficult, but fun, game to play. Keep at it and you will find your geography skills will improve. Have fun!
